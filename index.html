Unit 1 & 2 Vocabulary

Bit: A binary digit, either 0 or 1.
Byte: A sequence of 8 bits.
Roundoff: Error when not enough bits are available to represent a number with full precision.
Analog data: Values that change smoothly, rather than in steps (like pitch and volume).
Lossless: Compression that preserves all data and allows full recovery.
Lossy: Compression that discards some data, making full recovery impossible.
Metadata: Data about data, like information about a file.

Streaming Technology

Streaming platforms like Netflix and YouTube don‚Äôt send an entire video at once. Instead, they use a method called adaptive streaming where videos are broken into tiny chunks that are sent as you watch. This allows the video to adjust its quality depending on your internet speed so playback stays smooth.

To make streaming efficient, videos are compressed using lossy compression so the file sizes are smaller. Streaming services also use content delivery networks (CDNs), which store copies of videos on servers worldwide. This way, you get the video from the nearest server instead of one far away, making it faster and reducing buffering.

Fun Fact: Netflix can make up more than 15% of global internet traffic during peak hours, and YouTube streams over 1 billion hours of video every day!
